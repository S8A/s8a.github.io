Books read in 2021

10 Jan 2022 – personal

One of my New Year’s resolutions for 2021 was to read more full books, and I did. Here’s a chronological list of full books I read during the past year. I’m committed to read even more this year.

  • Economics in One Lesson by Henry Hazlitt.
  • The Metamorphosis by Franz Kafka (1915).
  • The Strange Case of Dr. Jekyll and Mr. Hyde by Robert Louis Stevenson (1886).
  • Anthem by Ayn Rand (1938).
  • Pride and Prejudice by Jane Austen (1813).
  • Moby-Dick; or, The Whale by Herman Melville (1851).
  • The Three Languages of Politics by Arnold Kling (2019).
  • Animal Farm by George Orwell (1945).
  • The Law by Frédéric Bastiat (1850).
  • Brave New World by Aldous Huxley (1932).
  • Economics and Free Markets by Howard Baetjer Jr. (2017).
